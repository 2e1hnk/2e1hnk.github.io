About 2E1HNK

Bio

I was first introduced to amateur radio sometime in the mid to late 1990s via JOTA, I subsequently became licensed in 1999 with what was then a UK Novice Class B License. Many thanks to John, G8HJS (SK, 2015) and Terry, G3MXH for helping me through the Novice exam. I let my license lapse in 2003 after having not used it for a while - mainly because I was a student and had no money for amateur radio trinkets :(. Fast-forward to 2015 and I re-activated my license. My operating is somewhat of an eclectic mix - From home I typically operate FT8 (over a remote desktop connection to a PC attached to the FT-857d in the loft) and I also enjoy getting out portable for the RSGB FMAC and UKAC VHF/UHF contests. Aside from these, if I’m on my own in the car I often have a 2m/70cm rig scanning or a DMR handheld listening to a local DMR repeater.

Shack

Due to space and family-imposed constraints I operate a remote-by-default shack. The radios (ok, only one radio at the moment) is in the loft attached to a PC and I connect to it via a remote desktop client. At the moment there is no access from outside my LAN but this could easily be added. My intermediate license doesn’t permit remote operation so this is not essential.

QSL

QSL via LoTW is preferred, eQSL is acceptable.

I try to upload logs to LoTW regularly but if you’re eager for a QSL then please feel free to prompt me to upload, Twitter is probably best.
